My commitments
Read this every week. Let it land. Let it guide you back.

Be myself

  • Stop hiding behind pleasing others. Share authentic thoughts, even when uncomfortable.
  • Tough conversations are not bugs — they are features of life. Do not avoid them.
  • Take complete ownership. Do not blame dad, Hardik, Nachiket, Abhishek, Payal, or the past.
  • Having a bad opinion about something is not against dharma. It does not make you bad.
  • Let fear not drive you. Act from integrity, not from what others expect.
  • Wrong defence and opportunistic defence are the worst. Good is the opposite of right.

Love better

  • Find the courage not to hide anything from her. Be fully honest with those you love.
  • Listen to emotions and needs, not just words. Be present, not distracted.
  • Cherish her and make her feel special — today, not someday.
  • Never say "time nathi" to family. That signals they are not a priority.
  • Say "Have a good day" when they leave. Return home fresh — not drained and distracted.
  • Mom-Dad page lago. Do not share financial fear or work pressure with them.

Connect better

  • Start conversations with something non-work, interesting, relevant.
  • Make real eye contact. Be fully there, not in the next thought.
  • Replace complaints with NVC: observe, feel, need, request.
  • Do not be time-pressed. Let others finish. Do not rush to say your point.
  • Appreciate people proactively and specifically — not just as a habit.
  • Bhai — respect and observe, do not judge.

Think better

  • Think from a post-death angle. What truly matters in the long run?
  • Empathy is not fusion. Care does not require self-erasure.
  • Act from awareness, not duty. Awareness-based choice creates real discipline.
  • Do not react to short-term triggers — money tension, anger, uncertainty.
  • Do not doubt or compare yourself. Comparison is a killer.
  • Use the Vedic lens: Jad-Chetan, Karma-Fal. Everything can be understood through this.

Work better

  • Prepare for every meeting: outcomes, obstacles, how to overcome. Share agenda.
  • Conclude meetings clearly: decisions, actions, owners, information.
  • Work at L2. Do not get consumed by L1 firefighting.
  • Think first — do not GPT as the first thinking tool.
  • Protect deep work. Two 2-hour slots per week, minimum.
  • Delegate assuming people are smarter. Pass the driving to team members.

Be present

  • Smile more — at standup, when someone leaves, while talking.
  • Look good whenever you go out. Check the mirror. Say you look great.
  • Observe your surroundings. Be aware of the room, the person, the moment.
  • Stop next-task thinking during meetings. You can wait in the moment.
  • Things are improving. Do not hold only the half-empty view. Have utsaah.

Serve & give

  • Charity is a lifestyle, not just a gift. Make seva a routine, not an event.
  • Give your full attention. Give your expertise generously. Listen charitably.
  • Level up people around you — team, family, community.
  • Share weekly resources. Present or attend one event a month.
  • Read charitably: give every author the most favorable interpretation.
"When I imagine others getting upset or leaving, I notice fear and tension in me.
I choose to act from integrity rather than fear, even when I don't know the outcome."
